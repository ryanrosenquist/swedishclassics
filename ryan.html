The Swedish shrimp sandwich is a favorite - fresh, luxurious and perfect for both lunch and a light dinner. With peeled shrimp, boiled egg, mayonnaise and dill on a fluffy wheat bun you get a dish that is both beautiful and tastes fantastic. Often served with a lemon wedge and a cold drink on the side.

Servings: 2 people | Time: Approx. 20 minutes

Ingredients

    For the bread:
  • 2 slices wheat bun or other light bread
  • Butter (for the bread)

    • Toppings:
  • 200 g peeled shrimp
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs
  • 1 dl mayonnaise
  • Some red leaf lettuce leaves
  • 2 lemon slices
  • Cucumber slices
  • Tomato slices
  • Dill sprigs

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Start by boiling the eggs for about 8–9 minutes until hard-boiled. Rinse them in cold water, peel and slice into thin, even slices. Rinse the lettuce, cucumber and tomato thoroughly and let them dry on paper towels. If the shrimp are freshly cooked, let them drain well.

  2. Make the base: Spread a thin layer of butter on the wheat bun — it helps keep the bread moist and adds a mild, rounded flavor. Then place a couple of red leaf lettuce leaves on top. The lettuce adds freshness and creates a small barrier between the bread and the moist ingredients.

  3. Assemble the sandwich: Arrange the egg slices evenly over the lettuce. Spoon or pipe the mayonnaise over the eggs and top with the peeled shrimp. Be generous with the shrimp; they should cover the entire surface in a thick, juicy layer.

  4. Serving: Add cucumber and tomato slices. Top with a sprig of dill and a lemon wedge on the side. Optionally squeeze a little lemon over the shrimp and finish with a few twists of freshly ground black pepper.
